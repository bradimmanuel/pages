Stanford University ITAI Competencies Framework

Three tiers of AI proficiency across seven modalities — what staff at each stage can do, recognize, and teach.

← AI Modalities Matrix

This framework describes what genuine AI proficiency looks like at each stage — not as a reading list, but as observable behaviors. Each modality has three tiers: Aware, Practitioner, and Fluent. Staff don't need to reach Fluent in every modality — the goal is depth in the two or three modalities most relevant to their role, and honest Aware-level orientation to the rest.

Aware I can describe this modality, recognize when it applies, and explain a real example from my team's work. I'm not building yet.
Practitioner I can run tasks using this modality, evaluate output quality, recover when it fails, and teach a colleague the basics.
Fluent I design my own workflows, combine modalities, and know where the approach breaks down. Others come to me with novel use cases.
Not everyone needs to reach Fluent in every modality. Conversational AI and Language Generation have the fastest ramps — most staff can reach Practitioner in a single focused session. Predictive Analytics and Intelligent Automation are steeper and realistically require technical depth to reach Fluent. The goal is intentional: know your two or three priority modalities and go deep there.
